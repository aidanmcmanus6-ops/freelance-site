AI & Automation

Rebuilding a runaway AI classification pipeline

A document-classification system had turned into a budget problem. An earlier version of it quietly ran up tens of thousands of dollars in model and cloud overages before anyone was even alerted, because there were no guardrails on spend. We were brought in to redesign it from the ground up.

The problem

The classifier was reading entire documents end to end. Many were thirty-plus-page contracts, but only a small portion of each actually carried the information needed to classify it. The system was paying to process a huge amount of text it did not need, on an expensive model, with no cost alerting to catch the runaway spend.

What we did

We rebuilt the pipeline around what actually mattered. We trimmed the input down to the part of each document that carried the signal, routed the work to right-sized, lower-cost models instead of one premium model for everything, and added cost monitoring and alerting so a runaway bill can never go unnoticed again.

The result

Cost per document fell by more than 99%, from over a dollar per document to a fraction of a cent, while the system scaled from a few thousand documents per run to hundreds of thousands. The overage problem that prompted the rebuild was eliminated, and spend is now visible in real time.

Monitoring & Reliability

Monitoring that backstopped a multi-million-dollar launch

A high-visibility website launch, a multi-million-dollar project, needed real confidence that it would stay up and perform during its riskiest window: go-live and the days right after.

The problem

Big launches fail in public. Without real-time visibility, the first sign of trouble is often an angry customer or a flood of support tickets, and by then the damage is already done.

What we did

Ahead of the launch we stood up dashboards, synthetic checks, and tuned alerting so the team could watch uptime, performance, and errors live. If something started to slip, the team would know immediately, with the context to act, instead of hearing about it hours later.

The result

The team went into launch day with full visibility instead of crossed fingers, and the monitoring stack kept watch through the critical post-launch period when the most users were hitting the site.
